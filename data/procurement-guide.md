# Navigating the Procurement Maze

In edtech, "it's a great product" is only 20% of the battle. The other 80% is understanding how public money moves. If you ignore the calendar and the "magic numbers," your deal will die of old age.

## The Magic Numbers (Purchase Thresholds)

Prices aren't just about value; they are about what a buyer can approve without a board meeting.

| The Price | The Process (Typical) |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Under $5K** | **The P-Card:** A principal or department chair can usually just swipe a card. High speed, low friction. |
| **$5K - $25K** | **The Three Quotes:** You need a "sole source" justification or quotes from two competitors to prove fairness. |
| **$25K - $100K**| **The Formal Bid:** IT review is mandatory. Legal will read every word of your TOS. |
| **Over $100K** | **The RFP / Board Approval:** Expect a 6-12 month cycle. This requires a "load-bearing" champion. |

## The Sales Calendar: Time is Your Enemy

If you pitch K-12 in September, you aren't "early"—you're 9 months late for the budget.

*   **Jan - March (The Hunt):** This is when next year's budgets are planned. Be in the room *before* the numbers are locked.
*   **April - May (The Closing):** Purchase orders (POs) are flying. If you aren't in the system by now, you're waiting until next year.
*   **July 1st (The Reset):** The fiscal year starts. This is when the "Spend it or Lose it" money often appears.

## Higher Ed vs. K-12: The Key Differences

### K-12 is Centralized
You sell to the district. If the CTO says "no" because of data privacy, the deal is dead for every school in that district.
*   **The ScaleU Strategy:** Start with the Curriculum Director to build demand, but engage the CTO early to clear the tracks.

### Higher Ed is a Patchwork
Departments and even individual faculty have their own "P-Cards." You can land a dozen $3,000 deals before anyone in central IT even knows you exist.
*   **The ScaleU Strategy:** Use the "Wedge" approach. Land in a department, prove the outcomes, and use that evidence to pitch the Provost for an enterprise deal.

## ScaleU Pro Tips for Accelerating Deals

1.  **Do the Work for Them:** Don't ask your champion "how do I get approved?" Give them a 1-page "Sole Source Justification" and a "Budget Request Memo." They are busy; make it "copy-paste" easy.
2.  **Accept POs:** If you don't know how to process a Purchase Order and an invoice, you aren't in the enterprise game.
3.  **Stay Under the Threshold:** It's better to close a $24,900 deal in 30 days than a $26,000 deal in 6 months.
4.  **Have your VPAT Ready:** In Higher Ed, accessibility is a hard gate. If you don't have a VPAT, don't expect a seat at the table.
5.  **Multi-Year Pre-Pay:** Institutions love budget predictability. Offer a discount for a 3-year deal paid upfront. It helps their budget and your cash flow.
