# EdTech Competitive Landscape

## How to use this file

This file maps key companies by education segment. Skills reference it to provide competitive context without relying on LLM training data (which goes stale). Update this file quarterly.

## K-12

### Learning Management / Course Delivery
- **Google Classroom** — dominant in K-12, free, deeply integrated with Google Workspace for Education
- **Canvas (Instructure)** — growing share in K-12, strong in districts that want more than Google Classroom
- **Schoology (PowerSchool)** — integrated with PowerSchool SIS, strong in mid-large districts
- **Seesaw** — strong in elementary, portfolio-based approach

### Assessment & Testing
- **Renaissance (Star Assessments)** — benchmark and progress monitoring, massive installed base
- **NWEA (MAP Growth)** — adaptive assessments, widely used for growth measurement
- **Curriculum Associates (i-Ready)** — diagnostic + instructional, strong in Title I schools
- **Illuminate Education (now Instructure)** — assessment management and data analytics

### Adaptive Learning / Tutoring
- **Khan Academy (Khanmigo)** — free + AI tutor, massive brand recognition
- **IXL Learning** — adaptive practice across subjects, popular with parents and schools
- **DreamBox (Discovery Education)** — adaptive math, elementary-focused
- **Zearn** — math curriculum aligned to standards, free core product

### Student Information Systems (SIS)
- **PowerSchool** — dominant SIS, especially in large districts
- **Infinite Campus** — strong in medium-large districts
- **Skyward** — popular in smaller districts

### AI-Specific (emerging, 2025-2026)
- **Khanmigo (Khan Academy)** — AI tutoring assistant, huge distribution advantage
- **MagicSchool** — AI tools for teachers (lesson planning, assessments, feedback)
- **SchoolAI** — AI-powered student interaction tools
- **Diffit** — AI-generated differentiated materials for teachers

### What differentiates winners in K-12
- Integration with existing ecosystems (Google, PowerSchool, Canvas)
- Evidence of effectiveness (ESSA tiers matter for procurement)
- Teacher workflow fit (saves time on specific tasks)
- Free or freemium tier for teachers (bottom-up adoption)
- Standards alignment (state standards, Common Core)

### Common failure modes in K-12
- Building for administrators when teachers are the users
- Ignoring the procurement cycle and expecting fast sales
- Not having a FERPA-compliant data governance story
- Over-engineering when teachers want simplicity
- Assuming all districts are the same (rural vs. urban, Title I vs. affluent)

## Higher Education

### LMS / Course Delivery
- **Canvas (Instructure)** — dominant and growing
- **Blackboard (Anthology)** — legacy market share, declining
- **D2L Brightspace** — strong in community colleges, Canada
- **Moodle** — open source, popular internationally

### Student Success / Retention
- **EAB Navigate** — advising and student success platform, large institutions
- **Civitas Learning (now Anthology)** — predictive analytics for retention
- **Starfish (now EAB)** — early alert and intervention
- **Degree Works (Ellucian)** — degree audit and planning

### Online Program Management (OPM)
- **2U (edX)** — largest OPM, financial challenges in 2024-2025 creating disruption
- **Coursera** — platform + OPM services
- **Noodle** — unbundled OPM services
- **Academic Partnerships (Risepoint)** — focused on enrollment growth

### Assessment
- **Turnitin** — plagiarism detection, now adding AI writing detection
- **Proctorio / ProctorU / Examity** — online proctoring (controversial, market shifting)
- **Gradescope (Turnitin)** — AI-assisted grading

### AI-Specific (emerging, 2025-2026)
- **OpenAI / ChatGPT EDU** — institutional deployment of ChatGPT
- **Microsoft Copilot for Education** — integrated with Microsoft 365 Education
- **Various AI tutoring / writing assistants** — rapidly evolving, no clear winner yet

### What differentiates winners in higher ed
- LTI 1.3 integration (Canvas especially)
- Accessibility compliance (VPAT required)
- Rigorous security review passing
- Evidence from peer institutions
- Faculty champion development

### Common failure modes in higher ed
- Selling top-down without faculty buy-in
- Ignoring accessibility requirements until procurement
- Underestimating security review timelines
- Building for one institution's workflow and assuming it generalizes
- Not supporting LTI integration

## Corporate L&D

### Learning Management
- **Cornerstone OnDemand** — enterprise LMS leader
- **SAP SuccessFactors Learning** — integrated with SAP HR
- **Workday Learning** — integrated with Workday HCM
- **Docebo** — AI-powered, mid-market strength
- **Absorb LMS** — mid-market, known for UX

### Content / Skills Platforms
- **LinkedIn Learning** — massive content library, professional skills
- **Coursera for Business** — university-quality content for enterprise
- **Udemy Business** — broad content library, affordable
- **Pluralsight** — technical skills focused
- **Degreed** — skills intelligence + LXP

### Compliance Training
- **SAI360** — compliance and risk management
- **NAVEX** — ethics and compliance training
- **Traliant** — modern compliance training (harassment, DEI)

### What differentiates winners in corporate L&D
- Measurable ROI / business impact
- HRIS/LMS integration
- SOC 2 Type II certification
- Mobile-first experience
- Manager visibility and reporting

### Common failure modes in corporate L&D
- Not having SOC 2 when selling to enterprises
- Building content without distribution (LMS integration)
- Focusing on learner experience but not manager/admin reporting
- Pricing too low for enterprise (signals lack of seriousness)
- Not having customer logos / case studies

## Cross-segment trends (2025-2026)

1. **AI everywhere:** Every segment is being disrupted by AI. The question isn't whether AI applies but how it's differentiated in your specific use case.
2. **Consolidation:** Major acquisitions continue (Instructure, Anthology, PowerSchool). This creates opportunities for nimble startups but also platform risk.
3. **Evidence expectations rising:** Buyers across all segments increasingly require evidence, not just testimonials.
4. **Privacy regulation tightening:** New state laws, evolving federal guidance, and growing institutional caution around student data.
5. **Accessibility as table stakes:** No longer a "nice to have" in any segment.
